Why Study Astronomy?

Astronomy is a fascinating field of study that helps us better understand our place in the universe. It allows us to explore the mysteries of the cosmos and learn about the origins and evolution of our solar system and the universe as a whole.

Astronomy Facts

Fact Description
The Sun is a star The Sun is the closest star to Earth and is the source of light and heat for our planet.
The first person to observe the heavens with a telescope was Galileo Galilei Galileo was an Italian astronomer and physicist who made many important contributions to the field of astronomy.
The largest known planet in the universe is Jupiter Jupiter is a gas giant and the fifth planet from the Sun. It has more than twice the mass of all the other planets in the Solar System combined.
The Milky Way galaxy is estimated to contain between 100 and 400 billion stars The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy that is home to our Solar System. It is estimated to be about 13.6 billion years old.
The Andromeda Galaxy is the closest galaxy to the Milky Way The Andromeda Galaxy is a spiral galaxy that is approximately 2.5 million light-years away from Earth. It is visible to the naked eye in the night sky.
The first (1st) exoplanet was discovered in 1995 An exoplanet is a planet that orbits a star outside of our Solar System. The first confirmed exoplanet was discovered orbiting the star 51 Pegasi by Swiss astronomers Michel Mayor and Didier Queloz.
